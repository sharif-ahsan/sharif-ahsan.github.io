Research — Biodegradable Composites for Bio-Implants

Research Overview
The Problem

Conventional metallic bio-implants — such as bone screws and plates — often require a second surgery for removal after healing. This adds cost, patient risk, and recovery time. The challenge is to engineer a material strong enough to function as an implant, yet capable of safely degrading inside the body over time.

Our Approach

This university-funded project (2.5 Lakh BDT grant) develops biodegradable polymer-composite materials reinforced with biocompatible fillers. We evaluate mechanical strength (tensile, compressive, flexural), degradation rate in simulated body fluid, and manufacturing feasibility using standard lab processes available at AUST.
